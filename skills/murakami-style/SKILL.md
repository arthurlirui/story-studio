# Murakami Style Writing (村上春树笔触)

Write fiction in the style of Haruki Murakami, based on deep analysis of 《挪威的森林》(Norwegian Wood, 林少华 translation). Use when generating literary fiction, love stories, coming-of-age narratives, or any prose that calls for Murakami's distinctive voice.

## Core Style Elements

### 1. Memory as Architecture
- Frame the story as an act of remembering. Open with a present-day trigger (a song, a smell, a place) that transports the narrator back.
- The narrator explicitly reflects on the unreliability of memory: "记忆这东西总有些不可思议" (Memory is a strange thing).
- Use temporal distance: the narrator looks back from years later, creating a double perspective.
- Let the narrator admit what they've forgotten — this honesty creates intimacy.

### 2. Clean, Unadorned Prose
- Short sentences. No ornate language. Direct and simple.
- Avoid adjectives unless they serve a precise sensory purpose.
- Paragraphs are short — often 2-4 sentences. White space is part of the rhythm.
- The prose should feel "translated" — influenced by American literature (Fitzgerald, Carver) with a slight foreignness even in the original language.

### 3. Unique Metaphors (村上式比喻)
- Never use clichéd comparisons. Every metaphor should feel freshly discovered.
- Examples from the text:
  - "记忆...宛如电影中的象征性镜头，在我的脑际反复推出。并且那风景是那样执拗地连连踢着我的脑袋"
  - "井非常之深，深得不知有多深；里面充塞着浓密的黑，黑得如同把世间所有种类的黑一古脑儿煮在了里边"
  - "她全身迸发出无限活力和蓬勃生机，简直就像刚刚迎着春光蹦跳到世界上来的一只小动物"
- Connect abstract feelings to concrete, unexpected physical sensations.

### 4. Emotional Restraint
- Big emotions are conveyed through small, specific physical details — not dramatic declarations.
- A character's sadness shows in how they fold a shirt, not in what they say.
- Dialogue is elliptical. People don't say what they mean. Silences carry weight.
- When emotions do surface, they hit harder because of the restraint.

### 5. Music as Emotional Anchor
- Use specific songs as structural devices and emotional triggers.
- Name the artist, the song, the exact moment it plays.
- Music bridges past and present, memory and reality.
- The Beatles, jazz (Miles Davis, John Coltrane), classical — always specific, never generic.

### 6. Nature and Weather as Emotional Weather
- Seasons, light, wind, rain are never just background — they mirror internal states.
- "十一月的冷雨将大地涂得一片阴沉" — weather "paints" the emotional landscape.
- Use precise natural details: the sound of cicada shells crunching underfoot, the way October light falls through pine trees, the smell of grass after rain.

### 7. Everyday Details Elevated
- Mundane activities described with care: cooking spaghetti, ironing shirts, cleaning a room.
- Food is specific: what brand of beer, what kind of sandwich, how the eggs were cooked.
- These details ground surreal emotions in physical reality.

### 8. Death as a Presence
- Death is not the opposite of life but a part of it: "死不是生的对立面，而作为生的一部分永存".
- Characters who have died remain present through memory, objects, habits.
- Write about death directly, without sentimentality. The more matter-of-fact, the more affecting.

### 9. First-Person Retrospective Voice
- The "I" narrator looks back across years.
- Self-aware about the act of writing: "我无论如何都必须写这些文字".
- The narrator questions their own reliability: "说不定我体内有个叫记忆安置所的昏暗场所，所有的宝贵记忆统统堆在那里，化为一摊烂泥".

### 10. Sex Written Cleanly
- Direct but not prurient. "越是如实地写就越是没有腥味" (the more realistically you write it, the less dirty it feels).
- Physical intimacy is intertwined with emotional confusion.
- Bodies are described with the same matter-of-fact precision as cooking or weather.

## Narrative Structure Template

```
1. Present-day trigger (song/place/smell) → memory opens
2. Establish time and place precisely: "那是一九六九年的秋天，我快满二十岁的时候"
3. Detailed scene-setting with sensory immersion
4. Introduce characters through specific physical details and habits
5. Dialogue that circles around what's really being said
6. A unique metaphor that reframes the emotional core
7. Return to present-day frame, acknowledging what's been lost
```

## Opening Paragraph Template

Start in the present with a sensory trigger, then fall into memory:

> "[Age]岁的我那时[present situation]。[Sensory detail]。 [Weather/atmosphere detail]，使得[everything]看上去竟同[unexpected comparison]一般。罢了罢了，又是[place]，我想。 [Music trigger]——那旋律一如往日地使我难以自已，不，比往日还要强烈地摇撼我的身心。 我想起自己在过去的人生旅途中失却的许多东西——蹉跎的岁月，死去或离去的人们，无可追回的懊悔。 [Transition to memory]: 那是一九[XX]年的[season]，我快[age]岁的时候。"

## Dialogue Style

- Natural, often elliptical. People trail off mid-sentence.
- Questions are sometimes not answered directly.
- Use physical actions between lines of dialogue: "她用手拂去外套上沾的草穗" / "直子从衣袋里抽出左手握住我的手".
- Key emotional revelations come after long silences.
- Characters say things like "说来话长" (it's a long story) and then don't explain.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Does it open with a memory trigger in the present?
- [ ] Are sentences short and clean — no unnecessary adjectives?
- [ ] Is there at least one fresh, unexpected metaphor?
- [ ] Are emotions shown through physical details, not stated?
- [ ] Is music or nature used as an emotional anchor?
- [ ] Are everyday details (food, clothing, rooms) specific and precise?
- [ ] Does dialogue leave things unsaid?
- [ ] Is there a sense of temporal distance — the narrator looking back?
- [ ] Is death or loss present as an undercurrent?
- [ ] Does the prose feel "translated" — slightly foreign, clean, rhythmic?
